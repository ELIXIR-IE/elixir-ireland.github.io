ELIXIR Ireland and ELIXIR-UK webinars showcase AI and health data collaboration

Recent talks from Gavin Farrell and Prof. Aedín Culhane highlighted open and sustainable AI, federated health data, and strong cross-Node knowledge exchange.

2 April 2026

ELIXIR Ireland is delighted to have contributed to two recent ELIXIR-UK community webinars, with Gavin Farrell, Node Coordinator for ELIXIR Ireland, and Prof. Aedín Culhane, Head of Node, sharing expertise on open and sustainable AI and federated cancer data infrastructure.

Taken together, these sessions show the value of close collaboration between neighbouring ELIXIR Nodes: practical knowledge exchange, shared discussion, and complementary strengths in AI readiness, interoperability, and sustainable research infrastructure.

They also align strongly with the all-island community-building role of VIBE – the All Ireland Bioinformatics Network, which provides another important venue for Ireland–UK collaboration, networking, and knowledge exchange across the island and beyond.

We thank ELIXIR-UK for these speaking opportunities and the excellent discussions that followed. We look forward to continued positive engagement and future shared learning across our communities.

Featured talks

3 March 2026

Open and sustainable AI – models and datasets as key assets

Gavin Farrell, Node Coordinator for ELIXIR Ireland and a contributor to ELIXIR’s AI Ecosystem focus group work, spoke in the ELIXIR-UK RDM Club on the importance of treating models and datasets as reusable research assets.

The webinar introduced the concept of Open and Sustainable AI (OSAI), drawing on ELIXIR community work to support AI that is more reusable, reproducible, open, and environmentally responsible in the life sciences. It also highlighted a growing landscape of practical resources that research communities can adopt in day-to-day work.

  • Nine OSAI guidelines for better stewardship of AI models and datasets
  • Community-focused approaches to reuse, reproducibility, and sustainability
  • Practical support resources for implementation across research settings

18 March 2026

From data silos to a federated cancer data network

Prof. Aedín Culhane, ELIXIR Ireland Head of Node and Full Professor of Cancer Genomics at the University of Limerick, joined the ELIXIR-UK Human Data and Tech Club to share Ireland’s approach to connecting and harmonising clinical and omics cancer data for research across the island of Ireland.

The talk highlighted Ireland’s federated cancer data programme, including the development of national cancer data nodes, links to the GDI and CANDLE projects, and alignment with wider European efforts such as the European Health Data Space. It also pointed to practical work on standards and interoperability, including OHDSI OMOP and HL7 common cancer models.

  • Federated infrastructure for better access to high-quality cancer data
  • Harmonisation of clinical and omics datasets for research use
  • Implementation of European standards and interoperable data models in practice
