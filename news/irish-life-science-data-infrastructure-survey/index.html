Irish Life Science Data Infrastructure Survey — Q4 2025 Results Published

ELIXIR Ireland and VIBE publish the findings of the national landscape analysis of Irish life science data infrastructure needs, now freely available on Zenodo.

21 April 2026

ELIXIR Ireland and the VIBE – All Ireland Bioinformatics Network are pleased to announce the publication of the official results report from the Irish National Life Science Data Infrastructure Survey (Q4 2025), now freely available on Zenodo. Conducted between November and December 2025, the survey captured responses from 99 researchers who collectively represent a scientific community of approximately 515 individuals. The report evaluates 8 critical areas of national research capacity, providing a detailed evidence base to inform strategic investments in Ireland's life science research infrastructure.

What the survey covers

The report is structured into nine thematic areas, providing a comprehensive landscape of current capabilities and deficits across the Irish life science research community:

1. Demographics & Researcher Profile

Primary roles, wet-lab to dry-lab balance, research areas, and research groups acting as infrastructure providers.

2. Infrastructure Capacity & Hardware Gaps

Comparison with other countries, ratings of institutional and national compute offerings, HPC demand, and specific hardware priorities.

3. Data Archiving, Storage & Stewardship

Storage behaviours, database usage, essential national repositories, RDM support needs, and FAIR & CARE principles awareness versus implementation.

4. Sensitive Data & Ecosystem Integration

Types of sensitive data handled, data sharing challenges, collaboration methods, and impact of commercial sensitivity on partnerships.

5. Future Analyses & Software Quality

Current versus future computational analyses, AI assistant adoption in research coding, and research software quality management practices.

6. Technical Proficiency & Training Needs

Team expertise, programming language proficiency, priority training needs, preferred delivery formats, and ELIXIR resource awareness.

7. IT Maintenance & Workforce Policy

IT infrastructure maintenance, the hidden cost of fragmentation, and key policy themes for workforce recommendations.

8. Barriers & Community Sentiment

The biggest barriers to research identified by the community, illustrated through word clouds and direct researcher feedback.
