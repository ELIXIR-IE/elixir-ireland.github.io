UCD co-authors contribute to Nature Methods paper on Open and Sustainable AI

A strong ELIXIR Ireland contribution to a pan-European publication in Nature Methods.

2 April 2026

Denis Shields and Raúl Fernández-Díaz of University College Dublin (UCD), an ELIXIR Ireland member institute, are co-authors on a new paper in Nature Methods focused on Open and Sustainable AI (OSAI) in the life sciences.

The publication is a pan-European effort, bringing together expertise from researchers across more than 10 ELIXIR member states. It sets out crucial guidance for researchers who develop and apply AI models in life sciences and health research — with a particular emphasis on ensuring those models are reusable, reproducible, and environmentally sustainable.

As AI tools become increasingly embedded in biological and health data research, the paper addresses a pressing need for community-agreed standards. It provides concrete recommendations covering model documentation, code and data sharing, benchmarking, and the environmental footprint of large-scale AI workloads — all grounded in open science principles.

ELIXIR Ireland is proud to see UCD researchers playing a role in driving this shared international best practice. The paper represents the kind of high-impact, collaborative output that ELIXIR’s pan-European network is uniquely positioned to produce.
