ELIXIR Ireland's Largest Ever Delegation at ELIXIR Europe AHM 2026

Seven members of the ELIXIR Ireland team attended the ELIXIR Europe All-Hands Meeting 2026, marking the node's biggest ever presence at the flagship annual gathering of the European life sciences data infrastructure network.

10 June 2026

ELIXIR Ireland attended the ELIXIR Europe All-Hands Meeting (AHM) 2026 with its largest ever delegation, bringing seven members of the national node team to the principal annual gathering of the European life sciences data infrastructure community. The ELIXIR Europe AHM brings together node representatives, scientific leads, and technical contributors from across the network to report on progress, share knowledge, and engage with the programmes and platforms that define ELIXIR's collective work.

ELIXIR Ireland AHM 2026 Delegation

  • Aedín Culhane — Head of Node, University of Limerick
  • Laura Whelan — Deputy Head of Node, RCSI
  • Anmol Kiran — Technical Coordinator, University College Cork
  • Nicholas Cooley — Bioconductor Global Engagement, University of Limerick
  • Maria Doyle — Training Coordinator, University of Limerick
  • Gavin Farrell — Node Coordinator, University of Limerick
  • Alex Mahmood — GDI Ireland, University of Limerick

The scale of the delegation reflects the breadth of ELIXIR Ireland's engagement with the European life sciences ecosystem, spanning training coordination, technical services, sensitive data infrastructure, Bioconductor community work, and federated genomic data. The meeting provided a concentrated opportunity to present Irish contributions, participate in platform and programme discussions, and build on the collaborative relationships that underpin ELIXIR's network model.

RiboSeq Services in the Spotlight

Anmol Kiran (UCC) at the RiboSeq poster at the ELIXIR Europe AHM 2026.

Anmol Kiran, Technical Coordinator for ELIXIR Ireland at University College Cork, presented a poster on the RiboSeq suite of bioinformatics services developed at UCC. RiboSeq is a specialist resource providing analysis workflows and tooling for ribosome profiling data, a technique that enables researchers to examine gene expression at the level of translation and to characterise ribosome occupancy across the transcriptome.

The poster session generated strong interest from the broader ELIXIR community, opening conversations with researchers and node representatives from across Europe. This kind of direct engagement is central to how ELIXIR builds shared awareness of member node services, and the RiboSeq poster provided an effective platform for ELIXIR Ireland to connect its specialist tools with potential users and collaborators across the network.

AI and Multi-Omics Benchmarking

Aedín Culhane (UL) speaking on AI and multi-omics benchmarking at the ELIXIR Europe AHM 2026.

Aedín Culhane, Head of Node and Full Professor of Cancer Genomics at the University of Limerick, delivered a talk on AI and multi-omics data integration, with a particular focus on benchmarking and the importance of rigorous, properly structured evaluation in the life sciences. The session made the case that benchmarking is not a secondary concern but a foundational requirement for translating AI and multi-omics approaches into reliable scientific discovery.

Culhane drew direct connections between rigorous benchmarking practice and progress in high-impact areas including cancer research, where the quality of multi-omics integration and the validity of AI-driven analyses have concrete implications for understanding disease biology and identifying therapeutic targets. The talk called for structured, community-driven benchmarking frameworks and reinforced ELIXIR Ireland's commitment to open, responsible AI practice in the life sciences.

Workshop Engagement and GDI Discussions

ELIXIR Ireland members participating in workshop and working group sessions at the ELIXIR Europe AHM 2026.

Across the meeting, ELIXIR Ireland members participated in a wide range of working group sessions, community workshops, and cross-node discussions. The programme offered concentrated engagement across areas including training, platforms, research software quality, and sensitive data infrastructure, with members contributing to both formal sessions and the collaborative exchanges that characterise the AHM format.

A significant strand of engagement centred on federated genomic data infrastructure through the Genomic Data Infrastructure (GDI) programme. Alex Mahmood (University of Limerick), who is closely involved in GDI Ireland's technical work, contributed to working discussions on GDI's data access, interoperability, and national node development, bringing Ireland's implementation experience into the wider European conversation.

A Milestone for ELIXIR Ireland

The AHM 2026 marks a meaningful moment in ELIXIR Ireland's development as a node. The size and range of the delegation reflects a node that is actively contributing across multiple ELIXIR platforms and programmes, not simply as an observer but as a substantive participant in European life science infrastructure. The team returns with new collaborative leads, strengthened connections across the ELIXIR network, and a clear picture of where Irish expertise sits within the broader European landscape.

ELIXIR Ireland looks forward to building on the connections and conversations from AHM 2026 and to continued active engagement across the ELIXIR community in the year ahead.
