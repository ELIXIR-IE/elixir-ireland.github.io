Bioconductor in Africa: Ethiopia's First In-Person Course

ELIXIR Ireland's Dr Maria Doyle (University of Limerick) co-organised and taught Ethiopia's inaugural Bioconductor workshop, held in Addis Ababa from 25–29 August 2025.

24 November 2025

Following the success of an earlier Bioconductor course in Nairobi, the initiative continued building momentum across Africa with Ethiopia's first Bioconductor workshop, held in Addis Ababa from 25–29 August 2025. The event was hosted by the Bio and Emerging Technology Institute (BETin) in partnership with the International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA) and the University of Limerick, and co-organised with support from Bioconductor (CZI EOSS) and UL Global/Erasmus+.

The workshop brought together 26 participants — selected from more than 170 applicants — from universities, national research institutes, and biotech groups across Ethiopia. The cohort included MSc and PhD students, lecturers, and researchers working across agriculture, public health, genomics, and AI-driven biomedical research.

100% of respondents would recommend the workshop, and 92% rated it "Excellent" or "Very good". The average self-reported improvement in R skills was 4.3 out of 5.

What was taught

The five-day, hands-on programme covered:

  • R for data management, manipulation, visualisation, and reproducible analysis
  • Bioconductor core data structures (e.g. SummarizedExperiment)
  • Exploratory data analysis and quality control
  • Differential expression analysis with DESeq2
  • Gene set enrichment analysis

ELIXIR Ireland connection

Dr Maria Doyle of the University of Limerick was a member of the instructor team alongside Dr Yohannes Gedamu Gebre (BETin), Niguse Kelile Lema (BETin), Dr Helen Nigussie (Addis Ababa University), and Trushar Shah (IITA). The University of Limerick's involvement reflects ELIXIR Ireland's commitment to global capacity building in bioinformatics and FAIR data practice.

The workshop also served as the final Carpentries teaching checkout for two local instructors, Yohannes and Niguse — a tangible outcome for sustainable, locally-led training capacity in Ethiopia.

Looking ahead

The growing demand — 170+ applicants for 26 places — signals strong and sustained interest in bioinformatics training across Ethiopia and the wider African continent. Follow-on workshops are planned, with the next event held in Benin in November 2025.
